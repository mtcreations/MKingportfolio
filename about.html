About Michelle

Educator. Writer. Advocate.

Hello! I'm Michelle King — a Pittsburgh-based educator graduating in May 2026 with a Master of Education in Secondary English Language Arts from Point Park University. My path to teaching was shaped by a deep love of literature and language that began long before the classroom.

At Virginia Tech, I earned dual Bachelor of Arts degrees in English Literature and Communication Science, graduating Summa Cum Laude in May 2021. I was actively involved in the Silhouette Literary and Art Magazine, served as Peer Assistant for Learning at the Cook Counseling Center, and was recognized as a Nikki Giovanni-Steger Poetry Prize Finalist, with research published in Philologia.

From 2022 to 2024, I had the privilege of serving as Poet Laureate of Prince William County, Virginia — my home district. That experience galvanized my commitment to pursuing a career in education, where I could bring the power of language and creative expression directly to students.

I completed my student teaching practicum at Chartiers Valley High School in Bridgeville, PA, teaching three sections of 10th grade Advanced and Honors English — a total of 70 students. Prior to the formal practicum, I had already been serving as a long-term English substitute for my cooperating teacher, Mrs. Kelly Seigworth, giving me an unusually rich and continuous classroom experience.

I lead with authenticity, empathy, and passion — and I am actively seeking a secondary ELA position in the Pittsburgh area.
